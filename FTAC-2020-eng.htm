Urban And Environmental Applied Physics

Pre-requisites

This course assumes that students have already acquired some basic knowledge during their three-years degree, and in particular:

  • thermophysical properties of moist air
  • heat dispersion through the building envelope
  • fundamentals of acoustics
  • fundamentals of lighting technology

If necessary, students who are not already in possession of this basic knowledge can integrate their cultural background by using the teaching material (slides, handouts, video recording of the lessons) taken from the web page of the Environmental Technical Physics course held over the years past:

  • Fisica Tecnica Ambientale (A/Y 2019-20)

 

Flipped Classroom - 1st semester

The first half of the course (corresponding to the 1st cycle of lessons) is set with the methodology known as "flipped classroom": the didactic part of the teaching makes use of multimedia material made available on this website (NOT on ELLY or on other poorly expandable platforms), as better specified in the following calendar.
During online meetings orgainzed on MS Teams, students should report to the class after viewing and studying the material relevant to each lesson, which is made available one week in advance. In the virtual classroom the activity is carried out as a "workshop", based on the solution of concrete verification or design problems.
The teacher and the students themselves alternate on the blackboard, solving problems also in numerical form and carrying out project calculations and sizing.
Four "flpped classrooms" are planned, with reference to the following topics:

1 Energy requirement for heating and air conditioning of buildings

Verification of the thermal and hygrometric insulation of the building envelope
2 Noise pollution and acoustic classification of the territory - verification of compliance with the zone limits

Verification of passive acoustic requirements of buildings
3 Design and energy requirements for interior lighting of buildings

Natural lighting through windows
4 Design and energy requirements for street lighting

Lighting pollution, the "dark sky" initiative

Each of these lessons requires a didactical load of 5 hours: 3h for watching the video lessons and reading the material provided, and 2h of interactive online activity on MS Teams. So the total work load for the first semester is 20h.

Exam Project (Laboratory) - 2nd Semester
The activity carried out in the first part of the course is aimed at developing a project, to be carried out during the second part (from February 2021 onwards), which is assigned to groups of 2 students, and which constitutes during he main activity carried out in this phase.
Students are entrusted with the drafting of a urban planning project related in an urban context with high population density, for which they will have to produce the technical documentation required by the current regulatory framework, and more specifically the following two documents:

  • acoustics climate analysis (including assessment of noise sources, if present)
  • design of the new public lighting system

At each lesson, some students present the progress of their project and the whole class interacts providing suggestions and criticisms. 10 lessons of 2h each are planned, for a total of 20 hours.

The final aim of the laboratory is the production of two chapters (acoustics, lighting) of the A3 book, which will form the basis of evaluation.
