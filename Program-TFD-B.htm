Objectives
The course is aimed to the practical application of theoretical concepts already learned in the previous courses of Technical Physics and Applied Thermal and Fluid Dynamics A. The topic is focused on heat and mass transfer phenomena, and the application is referred specifically to mechanical industry applied to the productions of food and beverages.

Program
Rheology
General concepts of rheology. Generalized treatment of Non-Newtonian fluids. Non-Newtonian models: Bingham, shear thickening, shear thinning, power law. Analysis of capillary flow without Newtonian assumption. Rheological measurement. The capillary tube rheometer and the rotational viscometer.

Boiling and Condensation
Dimensionless parameters in boiling and condensation. Pool boiling. The boiling curve. Pool boiling correlations. Nucleate pool boiling, critical heat flux, minimum heat flux, film pool boiling. Forced convection boiling. Condensation. Laminar film condensation on a vertical plate. Turbulent film condensation. Film condensation on radial systems. Dropwise condensation. Freezing time.

Thermal and fluid flow transients: analytical and numerical models.
Development of a numerical code for the simulation of thermal transients based on the finite differences method, employing a visual programming language.
Infrared thermograph: from a purely qualitative tool to a precise instrument for the analysis of thermal phenomena and transients.

Numerical experiments conducted in the lab, employing either computer code written by the students, and the general-purpose program COMSOL. Students who choose the integrative activity can get certification of two additional CFUs by presenting a small thesis about this lab experience.

Technical applications in the food industry: system for exsiccation, freezing and concentration of food. Examples of plants.


Laboratory activities
The students will also spend some hours in the CEDI information technology lab, when developing their software, and making simulations using the COMSOL program.

Examination methods
Oral examination, plus 1 to 5 points assigned to the students who took part to the experiments in the lab, and presented a written report.

Prerequisites
